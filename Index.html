Backgrounds Text Forms Flexbox Animations Borders Float Hover
Backgrounds in CSS let you control the visual canvas of your elements. You can add colors, images, gradients, and patterns to make your page pop. Experimenting with backgrounds is an easy way to set the tone and style for your layout. CSS gives you the power to style text beyond just color. Change fonts, adjust sizes, add shadows, and play with spacing to make your content readable, dynamic, and visually appealing. Forms are how users interact with your site. CSS lets you style inputs, buttons, and labels to create clean, user-friendly forms that are both functional and attractive. Good form design improves usability and leaves a professional impression. Flexbox is a modern layout tool for arranging elements in rows or columns. It makes aligning, spacing, and ordering items simple — whether it’s a navbar, a card layout, or a dynamic gallery. Animations bring your page to life. With transitions and keyframes, you can create subtle hover effects, loading indicators, or playful movements that enhance user experience without being distracting. Borders define the edges of elements and help separate content visually. CSS lets you style borders with different widths, colors, styles, and even rounded corners — perfect for buttons, cards, or sections. Float was one of the original ways to control layout in CSS. It lets elements move left or right, wrapping content around them. While newer layout methods like Flexbox and Grid are often preferred, float is still useful for images, text flow, and legacy projects. Hover effects respond to user interaction, making your site feel dynamic. By changing colors, adding shadows, or triggering animations when users move their cursor, you can make elements interactive and engaging.
